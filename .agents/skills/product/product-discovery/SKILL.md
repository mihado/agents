Run these three skills in sequence. Load each one with the `skill` tool, apply it, then move to the next.

## Pass 1: Customer Truth

Load `mom-test` with the `skill` tool. Apply its interview framework to the current idea or feature:

1. What do we actually know about the customer's life, not what they say they want?
2. What specific past behaviors can we point to (not hypothetical futures)?
3. What commitments have they made (time, money, reputation) vs. compliments?
4. Write or refine interview questions that follow the three rules

If you have existing customer data, process it through the mom-test lens. Flag any data that's compliments disguised as commitments.

## Pass 2: Problem Framing

Load `jobs-to-be-done` with the `skill` tool. Define the job the customer is hiring for:

1. What progress is the customer trying to make?
2. What circumstance are they in when the need arises?
3. What are they currently hiring to do the job? (competitors, workarounds, doing nothing)
4. What are the functional, emotional, and social dimensions of the job?
5. Write a job statement: "When [situation], I want to [motivation], so I can [expected outcome]"

## Pass 3: Positioning

Load `obviously-awesome` with the `skill` tool. Frame how this wins in the customer's mind:

1. Competitive alternatives — what would the customer use if this didn't exist?
2. Unique attributes — what do we have that alternatives don't?
3. Value mapping — how do those attributes translate to customer value?
4. Target customers — who cares most about that value?
5. Market category — do we fit in an existing one, or create a new one?

## Output

After all three passes, produce a discovery brief:

- **Customer truth**: What we know vs. what we're assuming (flag gaps)
- **Job statement**: The core job being hired for
- **Positioning canvas**: Alternatives, unique attributes, value, target, category
- **Next steps**: What to validate next, and how
